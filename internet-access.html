Policy 01

Clear the path for new ISPs

Reliable internet is no longer optional. Anchorage can make it easier to build, compare, and improve service so District 5 residents are not stuck with slow, expensive, or unreliable options.

The mission

The goal is simple: more competition, better reliability, and clearer accountability. Charlie will push Anchorage to make the city easier to build in, harder to ignore, and clearer about who is getting decent service.

Reduce local barriers

Streamline right-of-way permits, street-cut coordination, inspections, and utility work where local process slows down responsible infrastructure.

Build last-mile partnerships

Focus city coordination on last-mile connectivity, apartment buildings, small businesses, and neighborhoods where residents have few practical choices.

Set service expectations

Push for plain-language reporting on outages, service quality, and response times so residents can compare providers and advocate for fixes.

Use public dollars carefully

Align municipal projects with state and federal broadband dollars so trenching, roadwork, and public assets are not missed opportunities.
