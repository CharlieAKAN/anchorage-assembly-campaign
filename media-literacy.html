Policy 02

Build media literacy with ASD

Students and families deserve practical tools for the digital world: checking sources, understanding AI-generated media, protecting privacy, recognizing manipulation, and knowing when a screen is shaping what they believe.

The mission

Charlie will bring the Anchorage School Board, ASD, libraries, youth programs, and community partners to the same table so media literacy becomes a visible local priority for students and families.

Partner with the School Board

Convene regular Assembly-School Board conversations on AI, misinformation, online safety, and student readiness for a digital civic life.

Prepare for AI media

Help students and families understand deepfakes, AI-written content, algorithmic feeds, and the difference between useful tools and misleading information.

Reach parents and elders

Use libraries, community councils, and school partners for workshops on privacy, digital footprints, scams, online safety, and practical ways to support young people.

Include every community

Build resources with multilingual, Indigenous, and neighborhood partners so digital literacy reflects the full Anchorage community.
